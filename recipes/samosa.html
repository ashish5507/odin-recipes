Samosa

Description

A samosa is a popular fried pastry, originating in India, and often enjoyed as a snack or appetizer.

It's known for its triangular shape, crispy outer layer made from flour, and flavorful filling. The filling typically consists of a mixture of spiced potatoes and peas, but can also include other vegetables, meat, or even cheese, depending on the region and preference.

Ingredients

  • all purpose flour
  • oil/ghee
  • water
  • salt
  • potatoes
  • chillies
  • onions
  • garam masala
  • chilli powder
  • tumeric powder
  • green peas
  • cumin seeds
  • ginger
  • raisins (optional for filling)

Steps

  1. In a large mixing bowl, add all-purpose flour, a pinch of salt, and a few tablespoons of oil or ghee. The fat is what gives the crust its flaky texture.
  2. now, we are gonna use this dough to create samosa outer layering which we are gonna deep fry after filling it up
  3. Rub the oil into the flour using your fingers. The texture should feel a little crumbly and sandy.
  4. Slowly add water, a little at a time, and knead the mixture into a firm but pliable dough. It should not be soft or sticky. The firmer the dough, the crispier the samosa will be.
  5. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes while you prepare the filling.
  6. Peel and mash the boiled potatoes into small chunks—not too smooth. This gives the filling texture.
  7. In a pan, heat a tablespoon of oil and add a pinch of cumin seeds. Let them crackle.
  8. Add the mashed potatoes and green peas to the pan, along with a sprinkle of salt, chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. You can also add a pinch of dry mango powder (amchur) or a squeeze of lemon juice for tang.
  9. Stir everything together well and cook for 2–3 minutes so the flavors mix evenly. Taste and adjust the seasoning.
  10. Turn off the heat and let the mixture cool to room temperature before filling the samosas.

Shape the samosas

  1. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each one into a smooth ball.
  2. Roll each ball into a thin oval or circle, about 6 inches across. Use a rolling pin and a little flour to prevent sticking.
  3. Cut the rolled-out dough in half to form two semi-circles.
  4. Take one half and shape it into a cone by folding the straight edge inward and sealing it with a touch of water. Press the edges firmly so it holds.
  5. Fill the cone with a few spoonfuls of the potato mixture. Be careful not to overfill.
  6. Moisten the open edge with water and press it together to seal the top. Make sure it's completely sealed to prevent it from bursting while frying.

Fry the samosas

  1. Heat oil in a deep frying pan on medium-low heat. If the oil is too hot, the outside will brown too quickly and the inside may stay uncooked.
  2. Carefully place the samosas into the oil, a few at a time. Do not overcrowd the pan.
  3. Fry gently, turning occasionally, until all sides are evenly golden brown. This may take 8–10 minutes per batch.
  4. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
